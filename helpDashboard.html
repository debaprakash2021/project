Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum wage for unskilled workers?

The minimum wage varies by state and sector. As of 2024, the national floor level minimum wage is ‚Çπ178 per day, but most states have higher rates (typically ‚Çπ250-‚Çπ400 for unskilled workers). Check your state's labour department website for exact rates.

How many hours can I be made to work in a day?

As per the Factories Act, the maximum working hours are:

  • 9 hours per day (including 1 hour break)
  • 48 hours per week
  • Overtime must be paid at double the normal wage rate

What should I do if my employer doesn't pay my wages?

Follow these steps:

  1. First try talking to your employer or supervisor
  2. If unpaid for over 2 months, file a complaint at the local Labour Commissioner's office
  3. You can also file online through this portal's complaint section
  4. Keep records of your work days and any payment receipts

Call the wage complaint helpline: 1800-345-678

Am I entitled to paid leave?

Yes, most workers are entitled to:

  • 1 day of paid leave for every 20 days worked (after 240 days of service)
  • 12 days of casual leave per year
  • 15 days of sick leave with medical certificate
  • Maternity leave of 26 weeks for women workers

What safety equipment should my employer provide?

Depending on your work, employers must provide:

Construction Workers:

  • Hard hats
  • Safety harnesses
  • Dust masks
  • Safety shoes

Factory Workers:

  • Protective goggles
  • Ear protection
  • Gloves
  • Aprons
